PATRICK SULLIVAN

My name is Patrick, and I have a lot of feelings about games.

I'm a queer writer, game designer, Unity3D developer, and freelance editor currently under contract for the Atlus USA localization team. My editing credits include Caligula (2017) and two as-yet unannounced titles.

I'm also developing a tabletop roleplaying game called Dungeonhearts, which will be available for playtesting in 2017.

I care deeply about storytelling and games as a story medium, believe in the human imperative to create, and drink way too much coffee.

Incidentally, I also hope you're having a good day.

Trip is a cyberpunk short story published in the 2015 edition of L.A. MISCELLANY, exploring the implications of easily accessible full-immersion virtual reality. [Download]

The Banner is a poetic ballad published in the 2015 edition of ATTIC SALT, my college's honors-students-only journal. I was not an honors student. They took it anyway. [Download]


Dungeonhearts is my current passion project, a tabletop RPG about the relationships between dungeon-delving adventurers and the trials they undergo together. Inspired by the design principles of the Burning Wheel, Dungeon World, the Powered by the Apocalypse engine, and the Shin Megami Tensei: Persona video game franchise, Dungeonhearts is my largest design undertaking to date, and will be available for playtesting in 2017.

Until that auspicious time, anyone interested in the project's details is encouraged to email me!
